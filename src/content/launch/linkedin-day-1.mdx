I grew up with a hockey rink in my front yard in Litchfield, New Hampshire.

My dad built it every winter. Plywood boards. Garden hose. We'd be out there until we couldn't feel our hands. That rink is where I learned that the game rewards preparation, discipline, and doing the work when nobody's watching.

I play beer league in Lake Tahoe now. Different rink. Same love for the game.

After years inside the financial services industry — including time at Fidelity where I watched advisors switch from fiduciary to salesperson mid-conversation — I built Wealth In Yourself. A flat-fee fiduciary firm for entrepreneurs and real estate investors. No AUM fees. No conflicts.

But I kept seeing something that bothered me.

Hockey players — guys earning life-changing money in a 10-to-15-year window — were getting the same generic financial advice as everyone else. From advisors who don't understand jock tax. Who don't understand escrow. Who don't understand what it means when your entire earning career could end with one hit.

Your agent protects your contract. Your trainer protects your body. But who's playing the financial position on your team?

That's why I built Top Shelf Private Wealth.

A flat-fee fiduciary built exclusively for professional hockey players. No AUM percentage. No commissions. Just financial planning designed for the specific complexity of a hockey career.

Your career is shorter than you think. Your window to act is now.
