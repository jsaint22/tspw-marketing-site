Let's do the math nobody wants to show you.

A hockey player earns $10M over a 12-year career. After taxes, agent fees, and escrow, call it $5M investable.

A typical AUM advisor charges 1% of assets under management. That's $50,000 a year on a $5M portfolio.

Over 20 years — just the first stretch of your post-career life — you've paid that advisor $1M or more when you factor in the growth you lost on those fees.

One million dollars. For advice you could have gotten for a flat fee.

Now run it at $20M career earnings. $10M investable. That 1% fee becomes $100,000 a year. Over 20 years: north of $2M gone.

Every specialist on your team gets paid for the work — your agent for negotiating, your trainer for conditioning. Why does your financial advisor get paid a percentage of everything you've saved instead of a fee for the work?

AUM fees were designed to benefit the advisor. Not you.

A flat fee means your advisor's income doesn't change based on how much money you have. Their only incentive is doing what's right for you.

That's how Top Shelf Private Wealth works. Flat fee. No AUM. No conflicts.

Life-changing money should change your life. Not your advisor's.
